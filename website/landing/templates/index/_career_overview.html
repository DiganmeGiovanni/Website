Career overview

My journey begun when I was 17 (late of 2010). I chosen “Computer Systems Engineering” as my career at the university and after a few months I was completely convinced that it was the right chose for me. At those days I was actively reading/learning about linux, node, java, html5, google app engine and practically every technology that a found on internet, at the same time I was being formally instructed in software engineering, maths and applied sciences at school.

In 2014 (I have very clear this year in my mind), I went into my first end to end side project. It was a web application that plays multimedia content directly from multiple cloud storage providers such as Microsoft SkyDrive (OneDrive), Google Drive and Dropbox, something similar to Spotify/Youtube but private and with your own media. At that moment it was a really eye opener for me, I had to learn about REST Services, APIs, OAuth authentication, Http protocol, Http security, Scheduled jobs on server side, NoSQL Databases and frameworks for frontend development (Angularjs at that moment). Although the final version of the app that I created was very fragile, it was a great achievement for me and a motivation engine for the next years.
From that moment onwards I’ve been continuously working on side projects and looking for ideas to develop independently of my daily workload.

My formal professional career started at June of 2015 when I got my first job as a software developer at a local agency. I was working there three years creating web applications and learning multiple frameworks such as Laravel, Django and Spring boot and created a few android apps. Also I had the opportunity to coordinate a team to implement gitlab in our internal infrastructure and docker in order to increase the portability of our apps and speed up our development process. During my years here I got a lot of scenarios to learn and prove ideas to improve the quality of our developments and workflow.

At January of 2018 I was invited to found an enterprise focused on bring software development solutions to emerging local enterprises and businesses. Such as that adventure in 2014 that i mentioned above, it was a huge experience to a completely complex world such as project management, client meetings and presentations, team leading and effective communication between development, design and management teams.
The last 18 months have been unbelievable for me, I’ve been working on multiple very exciting and complex projects with multiple technologies and I’ve been working with awesome people from multiple disciplines and backgrounds.
